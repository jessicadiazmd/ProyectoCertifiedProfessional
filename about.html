How it started

To make the transition towards a low-carbon society a success, new sets of competencies are needed in the European job market. Informally and non-formally gained competencies, need to be formally acknowledged in order to become meaningful indicators on the job market. Certified Professional addresses both issues by certifying cross-functional competencies and by introducing a standard for future-oriented skills.

The Competency Framework is the backbone of the certificate. It defines informally and non-formally gained competencies by referring to practical experiences. Against this framwork an applicant's competencies are assessed. The Competency Framework builds on academic theories and concepts but is practice oriented in its configuration. It has been developed in cooperation with renowned experts in the field of innovation, entrepreneurship and transition and incorporates basic standards of existing competency descriptions and certification bodies.

The competency framework

Experts and employers agree current broad skill sets will not suffice to persist in future. Greater flexibility, experience in envisioning and implementing longer-term projects, as well as capturing the imagination of a variety of stakeholders will be needed.

Enter: Climate-KIC introducing a generic competency framework detailing competencies required by candidates to succeed in a more challenging and interdisciplinary work environment. Three core areas that will become increasingly relevant to a greener way of doing business are: Accelerating Transitions, Promoting Innovation and Driving Entrepreneurship.

A shared standard will increase the visibility and significance of related competencies; it will also increase transparency and unlock the European labour market. Climate-KIC's assessment process to introduce this new standard is Certified Professional. The trial phases of Certified Professional in Accelerating Transitions and Promoting Innovation have met with great enthusiasm.

Vision

In the European job market, future-oriented competencies have been undervalued. They do not usually form a part of formal higher education and are not formally recognised. Without formal recognition the talent present in this generation of European professionals remains hidden. Experts agree that these future-oriented skills will become vital to persist in a competitive global environment, faced with unpredictable and rapidly changing challenges. The focus needs to shift; creativity, systemic thinking, trans-disciplinary communication, and inspirational change management abilities will become increasingly important.

Certified Professional will highlight and recognise these future-oriented skills and tap into the hidden potential of the European job market. Introducing a standardized framework for new professional competencies will enhance innovation and entrepreneurial impact. This change is set to transcend corporates, start-ups, or public authorities across sectors and geographies.
